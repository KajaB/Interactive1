The History of Scroll Bars

What are Scroll Bars?

Scrollbars are the vertical bar on the right side of a window or a horizontal bar at the bottom of a window used to move the contents up and down or left and right.

The scrollbar is to allow the user to freely move through the page in order to view all the
content provided.

The scrollbar is the resolution to navigation complications.

Scroll Bar Properties

The thumb is the darker colored bar that changes in size depending on how much content is on
a page.

The track is what the thumb lives in.

Scroll Bar Usage

The scroll bar can be used by click and dragging the thumb, using the scrollwheel on mouse the arrow keys on keyboard, clicking arrows within the bar, and the trackpad of a laptop.

The Evolution of Scroll Bars

Xerox Star (1981)

  • Scroll arrows point inwards
  • Thumb is a fixed size diamond
  • + and - allowed the user to scroll
    from page to page

Apple Lisa (1983)

  • Page buttons
  • Arrows point up and down
  • Fixed size thumb

Macintosh (1984)

  • Track had gray area
  • Arrows point in opposite direction,
    towards content
  • Arrows scrolled one unit per click
  • Page buttons removed
  • Could click in track to move
    through page
  • Click and dragging thumb would
    also move through the page

Windows (1985)

NeXTstep (1989)

  • Scrollbar was moved to the left
  • Thumb is proportionally sized
  • Arrows sat at the bottom of the bar
  • Alt key moved content by window
  • Using the alt key while click and
    dragging the thumb slowed down
    its movement

Mac OS X (2001)

  • Thumb was proportional
  • Both arrows sat at the bottom

    apps on iPhones
  • Cannot interact with scroll bar like on desktop
  • Scroll bar in contacts allowed some interaction
